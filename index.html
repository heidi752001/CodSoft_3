Conditions for admission to military colleges

The student is of Egyptian nationality

From parents and grandparents who have this nationality, and the student or his parents must not have acquired other nationalities.

The student is not married

He has never been married and pledges not to marry throughout his studies at any military/civilian (college - institute - school).

The student is medically fit

In accordance with the conditions of medical fitness stipulated by the Medical Commission of the Armed Forces.

The student who has obtained the qualification

Sign the declaration in the file that includes the student’s treatment based on the military academic degree he obtained and not the university qualification.

What People Say about:

Military Colleges

Military colleges are distinguished from others in that they bear not only the full tuition fees for the student, but also bear his subsistence and health care, and the military college student hardly costs his father a single pound for education. He guarantees work upon graduation, as it is automatically distributed to the military unit in which he will work. He does not search for work after graduation. The army provides him with an apartment to live in, a car, durable goods, and everything necessary for his marriage, with a limited portion of his salary. The state provides him and his family with free health care, summer resorts, and social clubs at nominal prices. He enjoys social protection thanks to the wasps he carries on his shoulder.
